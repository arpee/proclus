Decentralized Search

What is a Decentralized Search Engine?

In a decentralized search engine, you send search requests to applications that sit (perhaps even anonymously) on the blockchain.

This engine is one such application. While it is not exactly sophisticated, what it is doing is new: handling requests as zero-fee transactions propagated across the Saito network. We handle search this way as it is faster than writing data to the blockchain. In situations where peer-to-peer connections are firewalled or otherwise unstable it is possible to send/receive data directly on the blockchain.

As the industry moves away from centralized search engines like Google, we expect blockchains to handle peer-to-peer discovery ("what is your IP address?" / "here I am, please connect directly") and payments ("here is my monthly micropayment"). Search engines will compete for inclusion in the browser and a variety of business models will emerge -- at least some search engines will almost certainly handle search requests for free in exchange for the privilege of receiving fee-paying Saito transactions directly from users (i.e. in exchange for a competitive advantage in Saito block production).

We consider this a positive vision for the future. Society will benfit as search engines lose their ability to censor users and repress free speech. Niche companies and talented individuals will be able to innovate in the search industry again. And barriers to the free flow of information will fall as political and corporate censorship becomes more difficult to accomplish.
